Why Sema is different

Others have thought about this. Here's the gap we're actually closing.

Translator apps exist. "AI for African languages" initiatives exist. Sema is built where most of them stop.

A live conversation, not a phrase lookup

Most apps handle one phrase at a time. Sema maintains a full conversation thread — each person speaks naturally, Sema tracks context, and both sides hear real, contextual responses.

vs. single-phrase translator apps

Starts with the overlooked languages first

Giriama, Pokomo, Rendille, Orma — languages spoken by hundreds of thousands but absent from every major AI tool. Sema names them explicitly from day one, not as a future afterthought.

vs. population-first roadmaps

Designed to work without a network

Cloud translation fails in rural Kilifi, Tana River, or the northeast the moment signal drops. Sema's core models run on-device — it works precisely where big-tech solutions quietly stop.

vs. cloud-only translation tools

Honest about what it doesn't know yet

Giriama and Pokomo phrases are flagged as unverified — not guessed. This discipline is what keeps Sema trustworthy in healthcare and legal settings where a wrong translation causes real harm.

vs. tools that guess silently

How it works

Four steps. Built to run without a signal.

Core models run on-device — Sema keeps working in rural areas with poor connectivity, syncing improvements only when network is available.

01

Listen

A noise-tolerant mic captures speech in any Kenyan language. Handles accents, background noise, and code-switching mid-sentence.

02

Identify

An on-device model detects which language and dialect is being spoken — distinguishing Giriama from Pokomo, Dholuo from Maasai.

03

Translate

Neural translation converts meaning — not just words — preserving context, tone, and intent across the language pair.

04

Speak

Natural text-to-speech responds aloud in the listener's own language — at natural speed, with correct tonal patterns where applicable.

Rollout plan

A phased rollout — prove demand before expanding.

Each phase delivers a usable product before the next begins. Early funders get a working prototype, not a promise.

Phase 1 · 0–6 months
Foundation
Kiswahili ↔ English prototype. University data partnerships. Pilot in Nairobi & Mombasa. Native speaker validation.
Phase 2 · 6–12 months
Reach
Add Kikuyu, Luhya, Dholuo, Kamba, Somali. First Giriama and Pokomo community data partnerships on the coast.
Phase 3 · Year 1–2
Hardware Device
Handheld device pilot for healthcare workers and teachers. Maasai, Turkana, Meru, Kisii, Embu, Samburu added.
Phase 4 · Year 2–3
Full Coverage + KSL
All Mijikenda languages, Rendille, Orma, Boran. Kenyan Sign Language integration. 60+ language pairs.
